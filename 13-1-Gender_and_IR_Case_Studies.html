My research tends to focus on international policy-making which is really just problem-solving using rules

  • I’m fascinated by instances of the global community trying to solve a massive problem simultaneously at the domestic and international levels.


One particular policy challenge I have been studying for a long time is the disproportionate impacts of climate and weather catastrophes

  • In short, what should we do when countries experience disasters not of their own making?


SLIDE: And that led me to this story

What is the event?

  • Just before the 26th Conference of the Parties to the UN Framework Convention on Climate Change a weather disaster struck Malawi (muh-laa-wee)

  • Specifically, a severe storm destroyed the schools in a number of villages


Why is this international?

  • Research growing in confidence that climate change is increasing the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events

  • So, countries are being made to pay dearly for the behavior of other countries


Why is this political?

  • Many of the poorest countries who contributed almost nothing to the problem are being hit the hardest.

  • Countries with limited resources have to make VERY difficult decisions about where to spend to prevent this damage and where to spend to fix the damage


Why is gender important here?

  • Per UNESCO, before the storm, Malawi (muh-laa-wee) ONLY enrolls about 37% of eligible students in secondary school

  • In Malawi (muh-laa-wee), when serious storm damages occur, girls are:

    • Often kept home to save money for food,

    • Expected to work in the fields or by gathering water from long distances, or

    • Married off to older men.


So, bottom line:

  • Behavior by other countries creates damage for the developing world,

  • Developing world climate damages disproportionately harm women and girls

  • Harm to women and girls in the developing world tends to harm the growth potential for these countries

  • THEREFORE, we CANNOT explain development in the developing world without analyzing gender!


Does this case make sense?
